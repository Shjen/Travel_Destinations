Since the depression after World War II, Ramen has been a staple in Japanese diet. With many many varietys, it is very easy to choose your favorite! A little tip from a chef, choosing the correct Tare is key when reaching a symbiotic balance of flavors. My favorite is a tare made from duck jus.
Although Kimchii is not native to Japan, it makes ramen much more enjoyable! Kimichii originates in Korea, with 98% of (South) Koreans making their own Kimchi variety!


While Ramen is certainly a staple, don't forget about the an integral part of the tourism industry -- The Majestic Mount Fuji! Most know Mount Fuji as the Heart and Soul of Japan. At an elevation of 12,380 ft is is the tallest in Japan, making it stand out quite literally!
Though it is a tourist attraction for those that flock from around the world, Mount Fuji holds a spiritual place within the heart of Japanese people, who make the pilgrimage to it's peak to pray at the shrine and seek enlightenment.
